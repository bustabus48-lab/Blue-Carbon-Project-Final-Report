## 10.1 Original National Mandate
The assignment was established as a national coastal mandate to assess blue carbon ecosystems and generate decision-grade baseline evidence for mapping, monitoring, and management. In practical terms, the mandate required a country-level baseline orientation rather than a single-site study.

This national framing remains the scope anchor for interpretation of all findings presented in this report.

## 10.2 Adaptive Implementation Approach
Implementation depth was refined through an evidence-led sequence in which broad baseline obligations were retained while technical effort was concentrated where data maturity, ecological relevance, and operational readiness were strongest.

This is best understood as adaptive management within mandate: national scope was preserved, while detailed workstreams were staged and differentiated to improve technical quality and implementation relevance.

## 10.3 Why National Mangrove Extent Work Remained Broad
National mangrove extent mapping remained broad because it serves a different function from detailed site baselines. It establishes the country-level spatial reference, supports comparative prioritization, and anchors national planning and reporting logic.

Without this broad layer, later site-level interpretation would lack a common national frame. The broad extent work therefore remained a core national baseline function throughout scope refinement.

## 10.4 Why Detailed Baselines Focused on Selected Sites
Detailed socio-economic, ecosystem, governance, and monitoring baselines were concentrated in selected landscapes to match where field evidence and implementation interfaces were most developed.

This focus allowed the program to move beyond descriptive national mapping toward actionable site-level interpretation of pressures, readiness, and monitoring pathways. It does not imply that non-selected areas are outside national relevance; it indicates that detailed depth was phased where evidence could support defensible analysis.

## 10.5 Why Muni-Pomadze Appears Across Phases but with Variable Technical Depth
Muni-Pomadze remains part of the assignment landscape and appears in cross-cutting socio-economic, governance, and risk analyses. At the same time, later technical emphasis in some mangrove-focused workstreams is more limited.

The chapter evidence supports a clear interpretation: Muni-Pomadze retains program relevance, particularly for restoration and governance considerations, while extant mangrove-focused technical depth is differentiated relative to other landscapes. This is consistent with phased, evidence-led prioritization.

## 10.6 Why Keta Became the Project-Specific Pilot for MRV, Drone, and Continuous Monitoring
Keta is treated as the project-specific pilot because available evidence indicates stronger layered baseline assets and clearer operational pathways for pilot implementation. These include high-value mangrove baseline significance, identified monitoring units, and pilot-linked MRV onboarding traces.

In this report, “pilot” therefore means implementation depth and operational sequencing, not scope exclusivity. Keta is used to demonstrate how integrated baseline layers can be hosted, monitored, and iterated in practice.

## 10.7 How a National MRV Framework Can Host Project-Specific Baseline Layers
The national MRV framework provides the common architecture: governance roles, indicator logic, data structures, and reporting pathways. Project-specific layers then operate within that architecture where detailed baseline data are available.

Under this model:
1. national framework elements establish consistency and comparability;
2. project-specific hosted layers provide operational detail for implementation and learning;
3. pilot experience informs progressive expansion as additional detailed baselines mature.

This architecture-plus-layer approach supports both national coherence and practical implementation sequencing.

## 10.8 Implications for interpretation of the findings
Findings should be interpreted through a three-tier lens:
- **national** findings describe baseline breadth and framework architecture;
- **detailed** findings describe higher-resolution evidence from selected landscapes;
- **pilot** findings describe project-specific operational depth, not national completion.

Using this lens strengthens consistency across chapters, protects against overgeneralization, and supports clear communication to government, donor, and technical audiences.
