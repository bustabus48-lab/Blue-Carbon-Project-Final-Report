## 4.1 Method Framing
This chapter provides a concise overview of methods used across the baseline package. It is intentionally high-level and avoids reproducing full technical protocols already documented in source materials.

## 4.2 Geospatial Baseline Methods
The mangrove baseline evidence is based on high-resolution remote-sensing and classification workflows, supported by ground-truthing and participatory validation processes. The method narrative in available evidence emphasizes that updated extent values reflect improved detection sensitivity rather than implied ecosystem expansion.

## 4.3 Socio-Economic and Governance Methods
The socio-economic baseline uses mixed participatory methods, including focus group discussions, key informant interviews, community profiling, mapping exercises, and transect-based observations. Evidence packs indicate that social safeguards and inclusion considerations were built into field processes.

Governance and readiness evidence relies on comparative site assessment of institutional arrangements, participation dynamics, and implementation constraints.

## 4.4 Ecosystem Health Methods (Current Evidence Boundary)
Available ecosystem-health evidence in this draft wave is primarily from environmental pressure and monitoring-plan materials, including site stressor analysis and proposed parameter monitoring structures.

## 4.5 Monitoring and Drone-Linked Methods
Current evidence indicates map-anchored monitoring design with defined sampling allocations and periodic measurement cycles, alongside boundary assets associated with restoration/protection/plot areas.

At this stage, method statements are limited to what is visible in available files; no claim is made here about complete orthomosaic workflow packaging.

## 4.6 MRV Design Methods
The MRV evidence describes a layered design approach integrating ecological/carbon integrity, pressure surveillance, and governance/safeguard assurance. It also specifies indicator logic and dataflow concepts suitable for repeated monitoring cycles.

Where repo-derived MRV descriptions are used, this chapter distinguishes clearly between architecture/design capability and verified operational maturity.

## 4.7 Method Use Constraints for This Draft
- Methods are summarized as implemented or proposed only where explicitly documented.
- Pending components are flagged, not inferred.
- Carbon inventory method depth is deferred to the dedicated chapter outside this draft wave.
