# Scope Logic Note (QA)

## Purpose
This memo explains the scope evolution in defensible terms for later reuse in report drafting and QA.
It separates: (a) original mandate, (b) implementation refinement, and (c) current deliverable logic.

## 1) Original project mandate (ToR baseline)
- The ToR sets a **national coastal mandate** for assessing blue carbon ecosystems (mangrove and tidal salt marsh), including mapping extent, degradation drivers, carbon inventory, monitoring protocol, restoration strategy, and final synthesis reporting.
- The ToR phase structure is national in intent (7 phases), with explicit outputs for national cover maps and baseline-linked monitoring protocols.

## 2) How literature review + inception refined implementation focus
- The ToR requires a Phase 2 literature review and a signed-off inception report to refine methods, assumptions, timelines, and implementation pathway.
- The repo’s scope logic note states that literature review + inception evidence refined focus while preserving national baseline responsibilities.
- **Possible inconsistency / evidence gap:** in this QA pass, the inception PDF text could not be extracted in-environment, so refinement details are treated as stated in the scope logic note pending direct inception-text confirmation.

## 3) Why national extent work can coexist with narrower detailed baselines
- This is methodologically coherent: national mapping establishes the country-level baseline and prioritization frame; detailed socio-economic/ecosystem/carbon/governance baselines can then focus on landscapes with highest implementation relevance and available field depth.
- The ToR itself allows prioritization after national assessment, and source reports repeatedly apply site-differentiated analysis within a national program logic.

## 4) Why Muni-Pomadze appears in some phases but not all later technical phases
- The scope logic note states Muni-Pomadze was included early, but later technical emphasis narrowed where mangrove relevance was limited in subsequent evidence.
- Geo-social baseline language describing Muni-Pomadze mangrove cover as “insignificant” supports a shift toward restoration-oriented treatment rather than major existing-stock management.
- This should be described as **adaptive prioritization within mandate**, not scope abandonment.

## 5) Why Keta is the pilot for project-specific MRV and continuous monitoring
- Sources consistently identify Keta as the largest mapped mangrove concentration and as the site with strongest layered baseline + monitoring assets.
- The scope logic note therefore positions Keta as pilot for project-specific MRV hosting/continuous monitoring implementation.
- This pilot logic should be framed as operational sequencing, not as replacement of national MRV framework coverage.

## 6) How national MRV framework and project-specific hosted baselines fit together
- The MRV design report defines a national architecture (institutional tiers, indicators, digital platform, QA/QC logic).
- Project-specific hosted baselines (e.g., Keta pilot layers) are implementation modules inside that national architecture where sufficiently detailed baseline data exist.
- In short: **national framework = governance/standards backbone; hosted site baselines = operational entry points**.

## 7) Reuse wording (cautious, defensible)
- “The consultancy retained a national baseline mandate while applying evidence-led, site-differentiated depth in later technical workstreams.”
- “Keta’s pilot role reflects data maturity and monitoring readiness, not a change in national scope authority.”
- “Where site coverage narrows across phases, the reports indicate adaptive prioritization rather than a departure from the original ToR mandate.”
