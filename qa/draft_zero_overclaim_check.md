# Draft Zero Overclaim Check

## Purpose
This check identifies wording that may overstate national detailed coverage, pilot completeness, or data readiness, and proposes safer alternatives.

## 1) National detailed coverage overstatement risk

### A. Potential overstatement
- **Location:** Chapter 2 §2.2
- **Current phrasing (short):** “An integrated baseline is therefore needed to…”
- **Risk:** Could be read as if all integrated baseline components are already complete.
- **Safer alternative:** “An integrated baseline is required; in this draft wave, several components are complete while others remain pending for full integration.”

### B. Potential overstatement
- **Location:** Chapter 5 §5.1
- **Current phrasing (short):** “establishes a national mangrove baseline… reported findings indicate…”
- **Risk:** Strong but generally acceptable; minor risk of being read as complete for all blue carbon ecosystems.
- **Safer alternative:** “establishes the national mangrove baseline layer currently available in this report cycle.”

## 2) Pilot implementation completeness overstatement risk

### A. Potential overstatement
- **Location:** Chapter 9 §9.4
- **Current phrasing (short):** “pilot implementation provides an early operational test bed…”
- **Risk:** Could imply proven operational performance rather than evidence of implementation depth.
- **Safer alternative:** “pilot implementation is treated as an early operational test context, with maturity levels varying by component.”

### B. Potential overstatement
- **Location:** Chapter 10 §10.6
- **Current phrasing (short):** “clearer operational pathways for pilot implementation.”
- **Risk:** “clearer” may imply stronger operational proof than currently exported visuals show.
- **Safer alternative:** “more clearly documented implementation pathways in available evidence packs.”

## 3) Data readiness overstatement risk

### A. Potential overstatement
- **Location:** Chapter 8 §8.3
- **Current phrasing (short):** “continuous monitoring architecture has been defined”
- **Risk:** Acceptable but should remain explicitly planning-level.
- **Safer alternative:** “continuous monitoring architecture is defined at planning and baseline-layer level for key sites.”

### B. Potential overstatement
- **Location:** Chapter 9 §9.1
- **Current phrasing (short):** “supports a national MRV framework design…”
- **Risk:** Low; no immediate change required.
- **Optional tightening:** add “design-stage” modifier in first sentence for consistency with later prototype language.

## 4) Sentences currently safe and should be retained
- Chapter 8 statement that boundary assets are not equivalent to full drone deliverables.
- Chapter 9 statement distinguishing implemented components from in-progress capability.
- Chapter 10 framing that pilot is implementation depth, not scope substitution.

## 5) Recommended micro-edits (minimal rewrite)
1. Insert “in this draft wave” qualifier when describing integrated completeness.
2. Replace “operational test bed” with “operational test context” in Chapter 9.
3. Replace “clearer operational pathways” with “more clearly documented pathways” in Chapter 10.
4. Add “planning/baseline-layer level” qualifier to Chapter 8 monitoring-readiness statement.
