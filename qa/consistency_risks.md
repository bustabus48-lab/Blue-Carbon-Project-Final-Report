# Consistency Risks: Scope Evolution and Coverage Changes

## A. Where site coverage changes across phases
1. **ToR national scope vs later site-focused detailed baselines**  
   - Change point: ToR mandates national assessment; several normalized reports provide deep detail mainly for Songor, Keta, and Muni-Pomadze communities.
2. **National mapping vs selective technical depth**  
   - Change point: Geo-spatial extent is national, but detailed readiness/risk/intervention narratives become highly site-specific.
3. **Muni-Pomadze early inclusion vs reduced later technical emphasis**  
   - Change point: Scope logic note says Muni appears in earlier phases but not all later technical phases; geo-social report describes mangrove cover as “insignificant.”
4. **MRV described as national vs pilot implementation centered on Keta**  
   - Change point: MRV report sets national architecture, while scope logic note and related assets frame Keta as pilot for project-specific hosting.
5. **“Keta and Ada” monitoring document vs “Songor/Keta/Muni-Pomadze” framing elsewhere**  
   - Change point: Monitoring plan naming introduces Ada terminology that may not map cleanly to other site labels used in baseline reports.
6. **Salt marsh national mandate vs mangrove-dominant evidence density**  
   - Change point: ToR includes mangrove + tidal salt marsh nationally, but many detailed outputs emphasize mangrove-heavy narratives and selected landscapes.

## B. Reviewer confusion points + one-sentence fixes

### Risk 1: Scope authority ambiguity
- **Possible confusion:** Reviewers may think implementation detail documents are redefining scope.
- **Fix:** Add one standard sentence in methods/scope chapters stating that scope authority is ToR + inception + synthesized baseline evidence, while MRV repo/materials provide implementation detail only.

### Risk 2: Inception refinement not directly quoted
- **Possible confusion:** Claims about scope refinement may appear unsupported if inception text is not visible.
- **Fix:** Insert an explicit evidence note: “Inception PDF extraction pending in this repository view; refinement statements are treated as provisional until direct citation is added.”

### Risk 3: Terminology drift (Songhor/Songor; Ada vs Songor; Keta Lagoon vs Keta Lagoon Complex)
- **Possible confusion:** Site names may look like different geographies rather than naming variants.
- **Fix:** Add a one-page “Site Name Normalization Table” in annex/QA mapping each variant to one canonical label.

### Risk 4: Overstatement of national coverage for detailed baselines
- **Possible confusion:** Narrative may imply all detailed socio-economic/ecosystem/carbon baselines are nationally uniform.
- **Fix:** Add a recurring qualifier: “Detailed baselines were site-focused within a national baseline framework.”

### Risk 5: Under-explained adaptive project management
- **Possible confusion:** Coverage narrowing may be interpreted as inconsistency or omission rather than evidence-led sequencing.
- **Fix:** Add a short “adaptive prioritization rationale” paragraph linking national baseline outputs to phased site-level deepening criteria.

### Risk 6: Muni-Pomadze treatment may look contradictory
- **Possible confusion:** Muni appears in community/governance risk analyses but has limited mangrove extent in geospatial findings.
- **Fix:** State explicitly that Muni remains relevant for governance/restoration-risk analysis even where extant mangrove stock is limited.

### Risk 7: Keta pilot language may be over-read as exclusive focus
- **Possible confusion:** “Keta pilot” could be interpreted as excluding other landscapes from MRV framework relevance.
- **Fix:** Pair every “Keta pilot” statement with “within a national MRV framework applicable to multiple landscapes as baselines mature.”

### Risk 8: Salt marsh coverage may appear under-documented
- **Possible confusion:** ToR obligations include salt marshes, but evidence packs may seem mangrove-centric.
- **Fix:** Add a gap statement identifying where salt marsh baseline/indicator evidence is present, missing, or pending extraction.

## C. Terminology and scope labels to standardize immediately
- Canonical sites: **Songor Ramsar Site; Keta Lagoon Complex Ramsar Site; Muni-Pomadze Ramsar Site**.
- Scope labels: **National baseline mandate; site-specific detailed baselines; pilot implementation layer**.
- MRV labels: **National MRV framework** vs **project-specific hosted baseline modules**.
