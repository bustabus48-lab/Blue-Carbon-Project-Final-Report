## What improved after PDF normalization
1. **Inception-stage support improved**
   - We now have direct (OCR-limited) visibility into inception chronology, governance context, and method/workplan framing.
   - This reduces prior dependence on second-order scope summaries when explaining how refinement occurred.

2. **Ecosystem-method evidence improved**
   - The ecosystem draft now contributes direct method and sampling details (timing, sampling approach, parameter lists, sample counts).
   - This strengthens traceability for ecosystem-method statements in baseline chapters.

3. **Traceability map confidence improved for selected chapters**
   - Chapters tied to scope/method framing now have stronger primary-source support through normalized files.

## Sections now with stronger source support
- **Scope and evolution framing** (Introduction, Objectives/Scope, Method overview context).
- **Ecosystem baseline method descriptions** (sampling design, parameters, timing caveats).
- **QA scope logic statements** distinguishing national mandate vs site-focused technical depth.

## Remaining weak spots
1. **OCR quality limitations remain**
   - Spacing artifacts and duplicated segments affect quote-level reliability in both normalized files.

2. **Citation-critical values still require PDF-side verification**
   - Exact phrasing, some numeric strings, and table-level values should be checked against original PDFs before final publication.

3. **Non-PDF gaps remain unresolved**
   - Carbon inventory completion, drone visual outputs, EOSDA screenshots/exports, and final MRV operational evidence are still pending and continue to constrain full chapter finalization.
