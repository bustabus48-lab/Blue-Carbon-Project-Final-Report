# QA Checklist

## Evidence and consistency
- Are all material claims supported by source reports?
- Are all numeric values consistent with the latest approved source?
- Are site names, acronyms, and institutional names used consistently?
- Are contradictions flagged rather than hidden?

## Structure and synthesis
- Does the chapter answer a clear question?
- Does it synthesize across workstreams rather than stack summaries?
- Are transitions clear between geospatial, socio-economic, ecological, and carbon findings?

## Recommendations
- Are recommendations evidence-based?
- Are they specific enough to act on?
- Are they aligned with protection, restoration, monitoring, and governance needs?

## Audience readiness
- Can a policy maker understand the management message without reading the source reports?
- Are acronyms defined on first use and technical terms kept to the level needed for the main text?
- Does the text distinguish completed evidence from pending inputs and assumptions?
- If EOSDA or continuous monitoring is discussed, is the setup status stated accurately?

## Editing
- Is duplication minimized?
- Are placeholders clearly marked?
- Are figures and tables referenced in sequence?
- Are annex references included where needed?
- Are unresolved questions recorded in the issues log?
