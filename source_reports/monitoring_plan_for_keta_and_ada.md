# Monitoring plan for Keta and Ada

MONITORING PLAN FOR KETA AND ADA WETLANDS ECOSYSTEM



Keta Lagoon Complex Ramsar site and Songor Ramsar site and UNESCO Biosphere Reserve are designated Ramsar sites under the Ramsar convention. The sites serve both local and international conservation and ecological obligations. There is however, pressure on Ghana’s coastal resources through population expansion, drift from rural to urban centers, industrialization of coastal regions and districts. To safeguard the ecological integrity of these wetlands against the ever-increasing pressures, the Ramsar convention proposed management and monitoring process for designated Ramsar sites. Integral to these are the guidelines for the wise use concept and the maintenance of their ecological character. 

The recent assessment of the sites in December, 2025, under Ghana’s carbon project revealed information on ecosystem variations that need further monitoring to provide informed decision for sustainable management of these sites. Community change agents received training through hands on demonstration throughout the period of field data gathering. Data therefore can be collected by the change agents within the designated sampling stations close to their communities.         

Since the 2 Ramsar sites are large, sampling can therefore be restricted to 57 sampling sites in Keta and 23 sites in Ada. These sites can be distributed within the supervised GIS map prepared for the study areas, whiles taking into consideration the diverse ecosystem. A quarterly monitoring schedule is proposed for each site for gathering of physico-chemical water and soil parameters.

Protocols for field sampling 



General information

 

Community change agents should randomly determine sampling sites considering the supervised map prepared.

Data sheet should be completed with the following information on the top of the sheet;

Name of site

GPS coordinates 

Any descriptive information 

Date 

Time

Name of recorder 

If there are more than one equipment, sampling can be done by  2 teams. Later they can work as a team to collect other samples. 

Equipment name, model and information on any defect or accuracy and range must be stated.

Any variation in sampling strategy, addition and decision to change the designated sampling sites should be stated.     



Water Quality – physico - chemical 

Refer to the sampling site considering the supervised map prepared

Take GPS coordinates of locations and state the model of the device.

Take the following data of the water parameters and state the model of the device;

pH

Temperature

Conductivity

Salinity

Total dissolved solids

Dissolved oxygen

Oxidation reduction potential

Resistivity











Soil chemical and biological indicators

Refer to the sampling site considering the supervised map prepared

Take GPS coordinates of locations and state the model of the device

Gather the following data of the soil chemical and biological indicators and state the model of the device;

Nitrate

Phosphate

Potassium

pH

Moisture

Humus

Temperature



The field sampling methods and the data analyses described in the report are suitable for the monitoring of the sites. However, the strategy and the device, if varied, should improve the data collection. The data collected should be transmitted to a centralized point for analysis and storage. Backups for the data is important to avoid information loss due to system failure or corrupted drives. The result of the data analysed should be made simple to be used for the management of the sites. The overall goal of the monitoring is to provide adequate information for sustainable resource utilization of the sites.



It is recommended that community change agents receive further training especially the devices to be acquired for data gathering if they are different from what was used for the training.

Any drastic or unusual changes in the physical or chemical quality of water should be reported immediately to the management authority or the District Assembly.
