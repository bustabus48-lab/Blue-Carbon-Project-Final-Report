# Evidence Pack: Ecosystem Health Baseline

## Chapter Purpose (for later drafting)
This pack assembles currently extractable ecosystem-condition evidence and identifies where direct ecosystem-health source extraction is still pending.  
It is intended to support cautious drafting of ecosystem status/stressor narratives while avoiding unsupported biophysical claims.

## Core Findings (traceable)
- Across Songor, Keta, and Muni-Pomadze, sources report severe and accelerating ecosystem pressure from extraction, pollution, erosion/flooding, and climate-amplified impacts. (Source: `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — “1.0 Executive Summary”; “4.0 Environmental Pressures by Ramsar Site”).
- Keta condition narrative includes high mangrove degradation/fragmentation pressure linked to fuelwood demand for fish smoking, with associated erosion/siltation/water quality stress. (Source: `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — “4.2 Keta Lagoon Complex”).
- Songor profile emphasizes ecosystem alteration linked to salt mining expansion and hydrological/land-use conflict effects. (Source: `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — “4.1 Songor Ramsar Site”).
- Muni-Pomadze profile emphasizes transition-forest/wetland-edge charcoal/fuelwood pressure with culturally protected sacred groves remaining relative refuges. (Source: `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — “4.3 Muni-Pomadze Ramsar Site”).
- Community-reported ecological signals are consistent across sites: declining fish catch, increased fuelwood collection effort, visible erosion, and seasonal/flooding anomalies. (Source: `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — “5.1 Narratives of Decline”; “5.2 Interpreting Change”).
- Monitoring plan specifies routine ecosystem-related field parameters for Keta and Ada: quarterly water and soil physico-chemical sampling with 57 sampling sites in Keta and 23 in Ada. (Source: `monitoring_plan_for_keta_and_ada.md`).
