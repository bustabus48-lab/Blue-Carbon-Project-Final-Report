# Evidence Pack: Mangrove Extent Baseline

## Chapter Purpose (for later drafting)
This pack compiles traceable evidence for the national mangrove extent baseline and its site-level distribution pattern.  
It supports drafting of baseline extent/distribution findings and the distinction between national mapping outputs and site-priority implications.

## Core Findings (traceable)
- 2024 geospatial assessment reports total mapped coastal mangrove extent of ~30,025 ha (2.81% of 1,068,361.48 ha assessed coastal landscape across 35 districts). (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — “Executive Summary”; “1.1 Overview of the 2024 Geospatial Assessment”).
- Keta Lagoon Complex is the dominant mapped mangrove resource at 20,717.95 ha (~69% of mapped national coastal mangrove resource). (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — “Executive Summary”; “1.2 The Primacy of the Keta Lagoon Complex”).
- Songor mapped mangrove extent is 465.76 ha (~2% of national total), with strong degradation narrative linked to salt production expansion. (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — “Executive Summary”; “1.3 Songor and Muni-Pomadze”).
- Muni-Pomadze mangrove extent is described as ecologically “insignificant” in this assessment, reframing it as restoration-oriented rather than existing-stock conservation-oriented. (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — “1.3 Songor and Muni-Pomadze”).
- Report attributes discrepancy from historical lower estimates to improved method sensitivity (Sentinel-2 resolution, Random Forest classification, cloud-free compositing, indices, and ground-truthing), not sudden expansion. (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — “Section 2”).
- Participatory and field validation inputs include 319 reference points and participatory resource mapping links with 37-community socio-economic work. (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — “2.3 Importance of Ground-Truthing and Participatory Mapping”).

## National vs Site-Specific Distinction (must preserve)
- National output: full-coast baseline extent map and distribution/fragmentation pattern.
- Site-level interpretation: Keta = core stock concentration; Songor/Muni = high degradation or low extant cover contexts requiring differentiated interventions.

## Writing Constraints (do not overclaim)
- Do not interpret higher 2024 mapped area as ecological recovery without evidence.
- Do not claim Muni-Pomadze has zero mangroves; source language is “insignificant” at mapping scale.
- Do not generalize Keta socio-ecological dynamics to the entire coast.
- Do not merge mangrove and salt marsh extents unless salt marsh numbers are explicitly sourced.
