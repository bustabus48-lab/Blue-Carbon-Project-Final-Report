# Evidence Pack: Socio-Economic Baseline

## Chapter Purpose (for later drafting)
This pack consolidates socio-economic, socio-demographic, governance-readiness, and inclusion evidence relevant to blue carbon implementation design.  
It is structured to support evidence-linked drafting on livelihoods, dependency, social risks, and institutional readiness without drifting into unsupported policy prose.

## Core Findings (traceable)
- Baseline fieldwork was conducted June-August 2025 across 37 communities in Songor, Keta Lagoon Complex, and Muni-Pomadze using mixed qualitative/participatory methods (FGDs, KIIs, mapping, transects). (Source: `draft_socio_economic_baseline_report_august_2025.md` — “1.2 Methodology Overview”; `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — “3.0 Methods”).
- Reported livelihood dependency is high: over 70% of households depend on mangrove-linked activities (fish smoking, salt production, fuelwood harvesting). (Source: `draft_socio_economic_baseline_report_august_2025.md` — “1.3 Key Findings”).
- CREMA readiness is uneven: high interest in Muni-Pomadze, emerging in Songor, fragmented in Keta. (Source: `draft_socio_economic_baseline_report_august_2025.md` — “1.3 Key Findings”; `governance_institutions_and_crema_readiness_report.md` — “4.2 Site-by-Site Readiness Analysis”).
- Inclusion and safeguards gaps are recurrent: PWD and migrant exclusion, women underrepresented in formal governance, grievance systems informal in >80% of profiled communities. (Source: `draft_socio_economic_baseline_report_august_2025.md` — “1.3 Key Findings”).
- Demographic pressure profile includes youth bulge (>50% under 35 in field findings) and high regional dependency ratios (Volta 73.1; Central 70.0; national 66.0). (Source: `socio_demographic_profile_report_of_waca_blue_carbon_communities_report.md` — “1. Executive Summary”; “5. Population Structure”).
- Household structure and gendered burdens are central: large multi-generational functional households (field-reported 7-10 persons), high female-headed household rates in key districts (e.g., Anloga 46.2% vs national 35.3%). (Source: `socio_demographic_profile_report_of_waca_blue_carbon_communities_report.md` — “1. Executive Summary”; “6. Household Composition”).
- Governance risk linkage: exclusion and elite-capture pathways are identified as high-risk if CREMA formation reproduces current power asymmetries. (Source: `risk_assessment_and_safeguards_report.md` — “4.1 Exclusion and Elite Capture in CREMA Governance”).

## Writing Constraints (do not overclaim)
- Do not present social indicators as nationally representative; most are site/community-scope findings.
- Do not imply CREMA structures are already fully operational; readiness is mixed and staged.
- Do not convert qualitative perceptions into hard quantitative trends unless an explicit metric is cited.
- Do not assume all marginalized groups have identical risks across sites.
