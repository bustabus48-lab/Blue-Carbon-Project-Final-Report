# Evidence Pack: Recommendations Basis (Evidence-to-Action Bridge)

## Chapter Purpose (for later drafting)
This pack provides a traceable recommendation basis map: which recommendation directions are already supported by findings, and where evidence is still partial.  
It is for grounding Chapter 9/10 drafting in explicit findings rather than introducing new proposals.

## Core Findings (evidence-backed recommendation basis)
- Protection and management prioritization should be differentiated by site condition: Keta conservation pressure management, Songor conflict-sensitive restoration, Muni-Pomadze re-establishment/buffer reinforcement. (Source: `geo_social_mangrove_extent_baseline_report.md` — Sections 1 and 4; `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — Section 4).
- Restoration-only approaches are insufficient without livelihood/energy alternatives due to direct fuelwood dependence and poverty pressure feedback loops. (Source: `draft_socio_economic_baseline_report_august_2025.md` — 1.3 Key Findings; `environmental_pressures_and_community_dependency_report.md` — 4.2 and 8.0).
- Governance recommendations need inclusion safeguards to avoid elite capture (women/youth/marginalized groups) and to align with safeguards standards. (Source: `governance_institutions_and_crema_readiness_report.md` — Sections 3 and 4; `risk_assessment_and_safeguards_report.md` — Sections 4 and 6).
- CREMA roll-out should be staged by readiness: fast-track where social cohesion/readiness is high; sequence conflict mediation first in contested landscapes. (Source: `governance_institutions_and_crema_readiness_report.md` — “4.2 Site-by-Site Readiness Analysis”).
- Monitoring recommendations should couple ecological, pressure, and governance indicators with clear response protocols and data governance. (Source: `blue_carbon_mrv_report.md` — Sections 4, 5, 6, 7).
- Scope-consistent recommendation framing must separate national framework actions from pilot/site implementation actions, with Keta pilot use only where justified. (Source: `project_scope_logic.md` — “How the scope evolved”).
